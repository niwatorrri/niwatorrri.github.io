Introduction

An overdose is a biological response to when the human body receives too much of a substance or mix of substances. An overdose can be intentional or accidental. People can overdose on illicit drugs, alcohol, prescription medications, and many other substances. The video below introduces what happens when you overdose.



In this project, we are going to investigate the VSRR Provisional Drug Overdose Death Counts dataset to have a peek at the trend of drug overdoses across the Unites States and potentially inform national institutions of best practices and policies. The dataset reports provisional counts of deaths due to drug overdose occurring nationally and in each jurisdiction for every 12 month-ending period and grouped by specific drugs or drug classes. The period starts from January 2015 to April 2019. In addition to an exploration into the drug overdose death counts, we are also curious regarding what factors are correlated or influential on these count numbers. Hence, for each state we also gathered their annual incomes, unemployment rates, crime rates and population over the years, seeking potential relationships between demographics, social structures and drug overdoses.
